Gerrymandering

Saturday, March 28th

Speakers



Wesley Pegden
Associate Professor at Carnegie Mellon University

Wes Pegden is an Associate Professor of mathematics at Carnegie Mellon University, where he does research on theoretical problems in probability and combinatorics, often arising from questions in computer science and statistical physics. One line of his research has resulted in mathematical tools for subjecting political districtings to rigorous statistical analyses. The key ingredient is mathematical theorems which allow Markov chains to be used to make rigorous and relevant statements about outliers, without requiring knowledge of the mixing behavior of the Markov chains in question. Leveraging these tools, Wes served as an expert witness in the League of Women Voters v. Pennsylvania case, which overturned the U.S. Congressional districting of Pennsylvania as a partisan gerrymander, and the Common Cause v. Lewis case, which overturned the state House and Senate districtings of North Carolina as partisan gerrymanders.



Ellen Veomett
Professor at St. Mary's College

Ellen Veomett is a Professor of Mathematics at Saint Mary's College of California. Her background is in discrete geometry, and she got involved in studying the mathematics of redistricting in 2017. Her most well-known work in this area is her analysis of the Efficiency Gap, in which she showed how voter turnout affects the metric. She has also done an analysis of the Declination, and is working on various projects related to redistricting and to detecting gerrymandering.

Getting There

The main entrance to Fordham University is located at the northwest corner of Columbus Avenue at 60th Street (113 West 60th Street, New York, NY 10023). The A, B, C, D and 1 subway trains all stop at 59th Street/Columbus Circle. The campus is located one block west.

Talks will be located at Fordham Law School, Room 3-04:

    From Columbus Circle, head to Fordham University. Walk two blocks up from 60th St and make a left. Enter the law school building at 150 West 62nd Street. Take the elevator up to the 3rd floor - on the glass side of the building, walk left and down to room 3-04 (note that there are two entrances to each room).
